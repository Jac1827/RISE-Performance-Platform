# Active Adult Lease-Up Rebuttal Brief

Date prepared: March 13, 2026

## What I could verify

- The local dashboard exports in this workspace do not contain live KPI values. They are HTML shells that read browser storage keys (`rise_leasing_v5` and `rise_weekly_portfolio_snapshot_v1`) rather than embedding current traffic, tour, application, lease, or occupancy data in the file itself.
- The three active adult communities tied to your portfolio appear to be:
  - RISE Viera: 166 units
  - RISE at Nocatee: 178 units
  - RISE at Glen Kernan Park: 308 units

## Key facts you can use

### 1. These are lease-up communities, not stabilized comps

- RISE Viera was a 2024 delivery and held its grand opening in January 2025.
- RISE at Nocatee was a 2024 delivery and held its grand opening in January 2025.
- RISE at Glen Kernan Park is a phased delivery. Public reporting says first units were scheduled for summer 2025 and the remaining units for summer 2026.
- That means as of March 2026:
  - Viera and Nocatee are still relatively new active adult lease-ups.
  - Glen Kernan Park is still in active delivery and is not yet a fully delivered asset.

### 2. Industry benchmarks separate lease-ups from stabilized assets

- NIC reported active adult occupancy at 91.9% in 4Q25 across all properties, including communities still in lease-up.
- NIC separately reported stabilized active adult communities open at least two years at about 95% occupancy.
- If ownership is comparing your communities to mature 95% stabilized assets, that is not an apples-to-apples benchmark.

### 3. Upscale active adult is typically slower to lease than middle-market product

- NIC published comments from a Carlyle/Overture panel noting that higher-end active adult communities lease more slowly than middle-market communities because many prospects first need to sell a house.
- The same NIC discussion said rental velocity depends heavily on the market and the micro-location.
- That directly undercuts the blanket claim that "traffic should be higher" without accounting for price point, home-sale dependency, and submarket conditions.

### 4. Greystar does not appear to have strong active adult operating proof in these exact submarkets

- I could not verify any Greystar-managed active adult rental community in Jacksonville.
- I could not verify any Greystar-managed active adult rental community in Ponte Vedra.
- In the broader Viera/Melbourne market, I found one current Greystar-managed active adult rental community: Parasol Melbourne.

### 5. Greystar's nearest public local comp does not show a dramatically better lease-up story

- Parasol Melbourne:
  - 159 units
  - Built in 2022
  - Public listings last month showed 14 to 15 units available and "up to 6 weeks free"
  - That implies roughly 90% to 91% occupied/committed if the listing inventory was complete
- In other words, the closest public Greystar-linked active adult comp in your geographic scope still appears to be using incentives and still does not publicly show a clearly superior stabilized outcome.

### 6. Your platform does have active adult specialization

- RISE publicly markets a dedicated 55+ Active Living platform.
- RISE's active adult page lists three featured 55+ communities in exactly the markets at issue: Jacksonville, Ponte Vedra/Nocatee, and Viera.
- RISE publicly emphasizes curated programming, active adult amenities, and specialized resident experience, which is evidence of a purpose-built 55+ operating model rather than a generic conventional multifamily approach.

## Best rebuttal points for the call

- We should not benchmark a 2024-2025 lease-up, and especially not a phased 2025-2026 delivery, against fully stabilized two-year-plus active adult assets.
- NIC's national active adult data shows the correct comparison set for lease-up communities is closer to low-90s occupancy, not stabilized mid-90s occupancy.
- High-end active adult leasing is structurally slower than middle-market because many prospects must sell a home before moving; raw traffic volume alone is not the right success measure.
- Greystar does not appear to have an active adult rental operating footprint in Jacksonville or Ponte Vedra, so there is limited submarket evidence that they outperform there.
- Greystar's one identifiable public comp in the broader Melbourne/Viera area still appears to be offering concessions and still does not publicly demonstrate a clearly stronger lease-up result.
- RISE already has local, in-market active adult operating experience across all three target geographies, which is more relevant than national scale alone.

## Smart pushback questions to ask ownership

- What exact comp set is being used, and are those communities lease-up assets or stabilized assets?
- Are the comps rental active adult communities, or are for-sale 55+ communities being mixed into the comparison?
- Are the comps middle-market or upscale, and are they comparable on pricing and resident profile?
- For Glen Kernan Park, is ownership evaluating a still-delivering phased asset as though it were already fully delivered?
- What is the expected traffic-to-lease conversion standard for age-restricted active adult product in these specific micro-markets?

## Biggest limitation

- I could not extract your actual internal traffic, tours, applications, leases, or occupancy from the local dashboard files because the live values are stored in browser local storage, not in the exported HTML files.
